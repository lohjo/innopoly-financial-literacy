# finfy-literacy: Student Financial Intelligence and Multi-Agent Architecture Plan

Codename: **finfy-literacy**

One-line pitch: **finfy-literacy helps newgrads stretch their first paycheck with a gamified learning experience to educate themselves on financial literacy**

## Product Judgement

This should not be another WordPress website, budgeting app, or services platform.

The stronger product is a **gamified financial literacy experience**: newgrads complete financial literacy challenges (duolingo-style), compete in leaderboards within a real peer group and AI summarizes with a strict format.

Core principle:

> Newgrads complete financial literacy challenges, compete in leaderboards within a real peer group, and AI summarizes with a strict format.

## Target User

Primary user: **newgrads getting their first paycheck** and want to make responsible financial decisions.

First recommended wedge: **gamified financial literacy challenges** that are engaging and educational.

Why this wedge:

- The easiest and most accessible way for new graduates to better improve financial literacy and integrate financial planning immediately after their first paycheck
- Newgrads have a strong desire to learn and improve their financial literacy, but they often lack the knowledge and resources to do so effectively
- Current financial literacy resources are often dry, unengaging, and not tailored to the specific needs of new graduates
- A gamified approach can make learning about financial literacy more fun and engaging, while also providing a sense of accomplishment and progress

Secondary future use cases:

- **Financial literacy for high school students**: expanding the platform to target high school students and provide them with financial literacy education before they enter college or the workforce
- **AI video call**: finfy mascot with live AI video call feature like talking to a smart friend

## Scope Boundary

### In scope for hackathon MVP

- **Gamified financial literacy challenges**: a set of challenges that cover various aspects of financial literacy, such as budgeting, saving, investing, and credit management
- **Leaderboards and peer competition**: a system that allows users to compete with their peers and track their progress on the challenges
- **AI-generated summaries**: an AI system that provides users with personalized summaries of their progress and areas for improvement in a strict format
- **User onboarding and account management**: a simple and intuitive onboarding process that allows users to create accounts, track their progress, and manage their settings
- **Mobile-first design**: a responsive design that works well on mobile devices, as newgrads are likely to access the platform on their smartphones

### Out of scope for MVP

- **Financial planning tools**: while the platform will provide educational content and challenges, it will not offer personalized financial planning tools or advice
- **Integration with financial institutions**: the platform will not integrate with banks or other financial institutions for real-time data or transactions
- **Advanced analytics and reporting**: while the platform will provide basic progress tracking, it will not offer advanced analytics or reporting features for users or administrators
- **Social features**: while the platform will have leaderboards and peer competition, it will not include social networking features such as messaging or forums
- **Monetization and premium features**: the platform will not include any monetization strategies or premium features in the MVP, as the focus will be on providing a free and accessible educational experience for newgrads
- **Accessibility and localization**: while the platform will be designed to be accessible and inclusive, it will not include extensive localization or translation features in the MVP, as the initial focus will be on English-speaking users
- **Gamification mechanics**: while the platform will include gamified challenges and leaderboards, it will not include complex gamification mechanics such as badges, achievements, or rewards in the MVP, as the focus will be on providing a simple and engaging educational experience for newgrads

## Core User Flow

1. Newgrad logs in to the platform and is presented with a set of financial literacy challenges
2. Newgrad selects a challenge and completes it, earning points and progressing through the challenge
3. Newgrad can view their progress on the leaderboard and compare their performance with their peers
4. AI summarizer provides the newgrad with a personalized summary of their progress and areas for improvement in a strict format
5. Newgrad can continue to complete challenges, earn points, and improve their financial literacy skills over time
